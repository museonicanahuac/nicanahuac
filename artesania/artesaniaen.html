CERAMIC ART OF NICARAGUA






Craft products have a unique value, as they are the expression of the symbolic or ideological values ​​of culture, it is the people creating, crafts are the greatest popular expression of identity. The origin of the craft is linked to the settlement of Nicaragua. The first men who came to the country had a poorly developed culture, they were dedicated to hunting, fishing and were gatherers.

The main materials used in the production of handicrafts in Nicaragua are: clay, marble, stone, wood, hides and skins, cabuya, wicker, bamboo, tule, pita, textiles, nuts, corn husks, sea shells, coral, tortoiseshell, jícaros, stuffed animals, sugar, metals and recycling materials, among others. Nicaraguan Crafts have been a product that has been produced since our Nicaraguan ancestors due to the need to have utilitarian products in Nicaraguan homes. Nicaraguan handicrafts were needed for the kitchen, to dress, to wear shoes, to mount a horse, to sleep on it like the famous petates, handicrafts have not only existed in Nicaragua, but throughout the world since throughout the The world has had a need for utilitarian products in the home.

Nicaragua is considered a country that has the largest variety of handicrafts in the Central American region. There are different types in each town or city, that is, everyone creates their own. Among the main crafts are hammocks woven by hand with very nice and strong cotton thread, clay crafts such as cups, plates, pots, which are useful items in the home. Fabrics, gold and silver jewelry creating unique and beautiful pieces, black pottery, with Matagalpa and Jinotega being its main representatives, also stand out, marble stone is used by artisans to make precious figures and objects such as virgins, spheres, vases, doves, abstract objects, bracelets and painted stones, a classic in Nicaraguan handicrafts.
