NICARAGUAN GASTRONOMY

The beauty, natural and cultural variety of our country in the center of the continent, are combined with the hospitality of its people and with a history that moves and inspires. This is a land with a great cultural, multiethnic and multilingual richness where solidarity and optimism flourish and make Nicaragua a Unique, Original land.

Like other Central American countries, the fundamental basis of Nicaraguan gastronomy since its origins has been corn, which has countless uses and constitutes the culinary heritage bequeathed by the ancient cultures that exist in that area. In addition to this, Nicaraguan cuisine uses other local ingredients, typical of the tropics. However, they also took on components used all over the world, a fusion that contributed to the enrichment of their cuisine.

In Nicaraguan cuisine, typical traditional dishes abound. According to its cultural and geographical characteristics, each region has its drinks, dishes or desserts, which over time spread to other parts of the country and have also delighted foreign palates.

There is nothing more characteristic of Nicaraguan culture than its delicious food, which is something that any foreigner who comes to Nicaragua will be impressed and satisfied with. Nicaraguan gastronomy is so rich and varied, most dishes are based on corn and some basic g rains such as rice and beans, without leaving aside the different types of meat that are used. Gastronomy is a reflection of national culture and identity, allowing us to identify the wide variety of dishes that belong to our roots, such as the delicious vigorón, the nacatamal, the old Indian, the vaho, and unique drinks such as atole; exquisite and original desserts, which have emerged from the Nicaraguan table.
