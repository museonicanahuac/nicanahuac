ORIGIN OF THE MUSEUM NAME

The NÁHUALT names is one of the pre-Hispanic languages ​​with the greatest diffusion, extending from the center of Mexico to Central America. The word "Nicaragua" comes from "NIC - ANÁHUAC", which means "HASTA AQUÍ LLEGO EL ANÁHUALC" (HERE THE ANÁHUALC ARRIVED)", or"HASTA AQUÍ LLEGARON LOS NAHUAS" (TO THIS PLACE THE ANÁHUALC CAME) Another version is that it means "tierra rodeada de agua" (land surrounded by water).

Nahuatl or Mexican is a Yutonahua macrolanguage spoken in Mexico and Central America. It exists, at least, since the 5th century, although, when the South Yutonahua and the proto-Nahua were differentiated, it began to be possible to speak Nahuatl in the strict sense.

Nic-Anáhuac ('place surrounded by water' or 'place in the middle of water') 'place where [the kingdom of] Anahuac ends', which coincides with the cultural borders made by the Nahuatl tribes that migrated to the south. The Nicaraguans migrated to this area from northern regions after the fall of Teotihuacán, as advised by their religious leaders. According to tradition, they had to travel south until they found a lake with two volcanoes rising from the waters, that is, when they reached Ometépetl (Ometepe).

In addition to the Chorotegas, Nahoas and Marribios who lived in the fringe of the Pacific, there were the Matagalpas who inhabited the mountain ranges of central Nicaragua, and the Ulwa-Sumo, Miskitos and Ramas who inhabited the banks of the great rivers that flow into the Caribbean Sea. Nicanahuac, in the Nahuatl language (spoken by the Aztecs) means "up to here the Anahuac arrives». Anáhuac is a valley in the central zone of Mexico, which before of the conquest it had great lakes that were later dried up. So it can be said that the name of our country it was placed thanks to one of its great natural riches, water. with the step of time the word evolved in Nicaragua, given the difficulty of the Spanish to pronounce it.
